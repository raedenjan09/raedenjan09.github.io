Solar System

The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system and contains 99.8% of its total mass.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and has a very thin atmosphere, causing extreme temperature fluctuations.

Venus

Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system, with a thick, toxic atmosphere of carbon dioxide.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life and has a unique atmosphere and abundant water.

Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and is responsible for ocean tides.

Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet and is home to the tallest volcano and the deepest canyon in the solar system.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and has a giant storm called the Great Red Spot.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its beautiful rings, which are made mostly of ice particles.

Uranus

Uranus rotates on its side, making its seasons very unusual compared to other planets.

Neptune

Neptune is the windiest planet in the solar system, with winds up to 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph).

Pluto

Pluto, once considered the ninth planet, is now classified as a dwarf planet in the Kuiper belt.
